## **Daily Investor Brief Framework**

Goal:

* You are “the founder who *always* communicates.”

* Short, honest, **same time every day**.

* Calm tone, no hype: “Here’s what we did, learned, and are aiming at.”

### **A. Structure of the Daily Brief**

You can send this as an email, portal update, or even a dashboard card. Here’s a concrete framework:

---

**Subject line examples:**

* `Caelumetrics Daily Update – YYYY-MM-DD`

* `Caelumetrics – [Day of Week] Snapshot (Pilot, Raise, Ops)`

---

**1\. TL;DR (3 bullets max)**

Plain language, investor-friendly:

* **Progress:** “Signed LOI with X for produced water offtake (non-binding, terms in progress).”

* **Learning:** “Updated pilot CapEx estimate \+7% after vendor quotes; still within modeled band.”

* **Risk/Focus:** “Key focus next 48h: finalizing SAFE lead \+ regulatory meeting prep.”

This should be readable in 10 seconds on a phone.

---

**2\. What Moved Since Yesterday**

Break into simple buckets:

* **Product / Tech / Process**

  * e.g., “Completed bench test on Stage 3 precipitation; hit 93–95% of target purity.”

* **Commercial / Partnerships**

  * e.g., “Met with \[operator\]; they requested revised term sheet with volume-flex band.”

* **Operations / Build**

  * e.g., “Finalized two site-layout options; waiting on utility easement feedback.”

* **Team / Hiring**

  * e.g., “Opened search for Plant Operations Lead; 2 promising candidates in pipeline.”

Each bullet: 1–2 lines, no fluff.

---

**3\. Numbers Snapshot (Only the Ones That Matter Now)**

During the raise / early stage, this might be:

* **Runway:** `X months` (assumptions: current burn; soft-committed capital excluded/included clearly).

* **Pipeline:** `Y operators in serious conversation (Z with term sheets under review).`

* **Pilot:** key readiness % or milestone status (e.g., “Engineering design 70% complete; permitting pack 40% done”).

Later, when plants are running:

* Volume processed, recovery %, revenue, uptime, etc.

The rule: **no vanity metrics.** Only what actually moves the story.

---

**4\. Risks, Unknowns & Blockers (1–3 bullets)**

Show you’re not hiding anything:

* “Still waiting on \[regulator\] feedback on discharge classification; contingency paths mapped.”

* “Lead investor wants X term; we are negotiating to keep control in these areas.”

* “Vendor backlog may push delivery of component Y by 2–3 weeks; exploring alternatives.”

Key: you *name* risks and signal you’re on them.

---

**5\. Next 24–72 Hours – Focus**

What you’re intentionally doing next (framed calmly):

* “Finalize revised SAFE docs with counsel; circulate to lead \+ key angels.”

* “Lock in final vendor for Stage 4 equipment; decision tree documented.”

* “Prep for site visit on Friday: narrative, safety, data ready.”

This keeps investors feeling the flywheel is steadily turning.

---

**6\. Asks (Optional)**

Many days this will be empty. When present, be specific:

* “Warm intro request: operator with \[profile\].”

* “Looking for a seasoned plant GM with X background in West Texas.”

* “If you know anyone with deep Rule 4 / ZLD permitting experience, we’d love to talk.”

---

**7\. Tone Check / Human Note (1–2 sentences)**

This is very you:

* “Team energy is steady. We’re focused, not frantic.”

* “We had a good laugh today because X. Spirits are good even as we wrestle with Y.”

It reminds them there are humans (and a human-guided council of DIs) doing the work.

---
